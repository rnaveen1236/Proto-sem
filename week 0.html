Module 5

Formative Assessment-1

Generating a Business Plan & Presenting an MVP

Objective:

Students will work in groups to develop a comprehensive business plan (B-plan) and Minimum Viable Product (MVP). They will learn how to effectively present and pitch their ideas. This activity aims to enhance their entrepreneurial skills, including market research, strategic planning, and public speaking.

Instructions:

  1. Each Team will work on their chosen idea
  • Idea Approval: Facilitators may approve the ideas to ensure feasibility and relevance.
  1. Research and Business Plan Development
  • Market Research: Teams will complete their research on the market, target audience, and competitors.
  • Business Plan Components:
  • Executive Summary: Overview of the business idea.
  • Business Description: Detailed explanation of the product/service.
  • Market Analysis: Insights into the market and competitive landscape.
  • Marketing and Sales Strategy: Plans for reaching and engaging customers.
  • Operational Plan: Outline of the operational workflow.
  • Financial Projections: Basic financial estimates (optional for MVPs).
  • Develop MVP: Each team will focus on creating their MVP/ Functional prototype or a detailed mock-up of their product.
  1. Pitch Preparation
  • Pitch Deck Creation: Teams will create a presentation that includes (refer to the Bplan elements:
  • Problem Statement
  • Solution (Product/Service)
  • Market Opportunity
  • Business Model
  • Marketing Strategy
  • Team Overview
  • Financial Summary
  • Practice Pitches: Teams will practice their pitches, focusing on clarity, confidence, and engagement.

  1. Presentation and Pitch
  • Pitch Presentation: Each team will have 10 minutes to present their B-plan & MVP, followed by 5 minutes for Q&A from the audience and instructor panel.
  • Feedback Session: Facilitators and peers will provide constructive feedback on the presentation and the overall business idea.
  1. Reflection and Improvement
  • Review Feedback: Each team will review the feedback received and discuss potential improvements.
  • Final Adjustments: Teams can make final adjustments to their B-plan or MVP based on the feedback.

Assessment Criteria:

  • Problem Identification: Is it a good opportunity.
  • Creativity and Innovation: Originality and uniqueness of the business idea or MVP.
  • Market Research: Depth and relevance of market research conducted.
  • Business Model: Clarity and relevance of the model.
  • Business Plan Quality: Clarity, coherence, and completeness of the business plan.
  • Pitch Delivery: Effectiveness of the presentation, including clarity, engagement, and time management.
  • Team Collaboration: Level of cooperation and participation among group members.

Outcome:

By the end of this activity, students will have hands-on experience in developing a business plan or MVP and will gain valuable skills in pitching and public speaking. They will also learn to think critically about market opportunities and the practical aspects of bringing a business idea to life.
